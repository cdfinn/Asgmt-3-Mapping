Change in votes for Trump 2020 vs 2024

Donald Trump's reelection to the White House shocked the nation but just how much did he improve on his 2020 performance? This map shows the actual change in the votes Trump earned in each state in 2020 vs 2024.

   

Trump's 2024 electoral victory has been hailed as a massive 'red wave' by his political allies.
Illustration by Renee Klahr/NPR

           

by Charlie Finnerty // Dec 19, 2024

            "America has given us an unprecedented and powerful mandate,” once-again president elect Donald Trump said late on election night.

Trump has once again shocked the nation with a second victory, this time even securing a razor-thin win in the popular vote for the first time. For the last several weeks, pundits across party wings and the ideological spectrum have tried to make sense of what they present as a coast-to-coast embrace of Trumpist politics.

Election result data from Cook Political Report and CNN, however, show a fairly modest increase in the votes Trump actually earned compared to his 2020 loss. Nationally, Trump secured about 3 million more votes in 2024 than 2020, a difference of nearly 4%. Across the board, total turnout fell by almost 3% from the 2020 presedential election.
